Atheist

If you don’t know already, I am an unabashedly, unapologetically outspoken black atheist. That's a major rarity in the my demographic. Roughly eight-in-ten (79%) African Americans self-identify as Christian according to the 2014 U.S. Religious Landscape Study.

Since my specific demographic, a subset within the atheist community, is so small and under-represented, I feel it’s my duty to be outspoken and vocal about it. I want to show to my community that it’s ok to come out because there’s more of us then they think. Most people are raised to believe in the god of their parents and community, without taking the time to consider the issue themselves.

Atheism is NOT a claim to knowledge, it's a claim to skepticism. To refuse belief in a grandiose claim with no evidence.

Theists claim that there is an allmighty divine being responsible for the creation of the universe. Not only that, but the most popluar religions conclude that this is a personal god, directly involving itself with the affairs of humanity. An all-knowing, all-powerful perfectly moral being that watches over us and pronounces judgement over your soul after you die

Honestly the idea of a God looking out for me doesn't sound so bad. Of COURSE I would like to believe in a a soul and existing in some after-life, who wants to blink out of existence after a short time on this planet. However, the very nature of God makes it unfalsifiable. Meaning there isn't any possible way to prove or disprove god's existence. So there is no ultimate way of knowing for sure one way or another, making every last one of us agnostic to some degree.
